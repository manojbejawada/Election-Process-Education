Indian Election Timeline
The lifecycle of a Lok Sabha (General) Election in India.
📢
Phase 1
Announcement by ECI
⏱ Day 0
The Election Commission of India (ECI) announces the full schedule. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into force immediately.
  • Schedule Release
  • MCC Applied
  • Ban on new projects
  • Police deployment
📝
Phase 2
Notification & Nominations
⏱ Weeks 1–3
Candidates file their nomination papers (Form 2B). They must disclose assets, educational background, and criminal history.
  • Filing Nominations
  • Scrutiny of papers
  • Withdrawal period
  • Final list of candidates
📣
Phase 3
Campaigning
⏱ Weeks 4–6
Political parties release manifestos and hold rallies. Campaigning must stop 48 hours before the conclusion of the poll in a constituency.
  • Manifesto Release
  • Public Rallies
  • Door-to-door visits
  • Campaign Silence
🗳️
Phase 4
Polling Day
⏱ May 13, 2024 (Vijayawada)
Citizens cast their votes using EVMs (Electronic Voting Machines) with VVPAT verification. Polling Day for Vijayawada is May 13.
  • Booth Setup
  • EVM Testing (Mock Poll)
  • Secure Voting
  • Seal of EVMs
📅 Add to Google Calendar
📊
Phase 5
Counting & Results
⏱ Result Day
Votes are counted simultaneously across all constituencies. Result Trends are tracked on the ECI website. Winners are formally certified.
  • Strongroom Opening
  • Round-wise Counting
  • VVPAT Slip Check
  • Certification
Voter's Guide
Everything an Indian citizen needs to know before heading to the polls.
🇮🇳
Important: Voting is a right. Even if you don't have an EPIC card, you can vote with other valid IDs (Aadhaar, DL, etc.) only if your name is in the Electoral Roll.

Step 1: Check Your Name

1
Electoral Roll
Visit the ECI Voters Service Portal (VSP) to search your name in the electoral roll. If not listed, you cannot vote.
2
Form 6
If you are 18 and not registered, fill Form 6 on the NVSP portal or the 'Voter Helpline' app.
3
Booth Details
Find your Polling Station and Booth Level Officer (BLO) details on the app or portal.

Step 2: On Polling Day

1
First Polling Officer
Will check your name in the voter list and your identity ID (EPIC/Aadhaar).
2
Second Polling Officer
Will ink your finger, give you a slip, and take your signature in a register (Form 17A).
3
The Voting Booth
Press the blue button on the EVM next to the symbol of your chosen candidate.
4
Verify via VVPAT
Check the VVPAT glass. A slip will appear for 7 seconds showing your choice before falling into the box.
